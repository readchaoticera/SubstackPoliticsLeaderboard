About this leaderboard

  • This board only includes leading publications that self-identify under Substack's U.S. Politics or News categories. Publications that list themselves under other notable categories — such as World Politics, Finance, or Technology — are excluded, even if their coverage is political.
  • Paid subscribers are an estimate. Substack does not publish exact paid counts, so this shows the order-of-magnitude it publishes in each publication's ranking detail (e.g. "Hundreds of thousands of paid subscribers" → 100K+). The pill colour follows the Bestseller badge tier (white 100+, orange 1K+, purple 10K+). Hover a pill for the full description.
  • Total subscribers is the rounded total subscriber count (free + paid) Substack reports for each publication.
  • Lean is a subjective editorial judgment by Chaotic Era, not a scientific measure — reasonable people will disagree. We use three buckets: Left (left-leaning, progressive, or Democrat), Right (right-leaning, conservative, or Republican), and Neutral (nonpartisan). Anti-Trump conservatives are grouped by their current alignment, and publications we haven't assessed show as Unrated. Spot one you'd label differently? Let us know.
  • The # column is Substack's own leaderboard rank, from its public category leaderboards (U.S. Politics and News). Substack doesn't publish the exact formula, but it blends paid and total (free) subscribers — and likely engagement — so it won't always match sorting by Total Subs or Paid Subs alone. Click any column header to re-sort. Data is refreshed periodically.

* denotes rough estimate from industry reports, subscribers are hidden within the Substack platform.
